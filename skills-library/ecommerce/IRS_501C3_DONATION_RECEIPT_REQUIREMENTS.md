## Required Content for All Receipts

### Mandatory Elements (IRC §170(f)(8))

Every donation receipt for contributions of $250 or more MUST include these six elements:

#### 1. **Organization Information**
- Legal name of the 501(c)(3) organization
- Statement that the organization is tax-exempt under IRC Section 501(c)(3)
- Organization's Employer Identification Number (EIN)
- Organization's physical address

#### 2. **Donor Information**
- Donor's full name
- Donor's address

#### 3. **Contribution Details**
- Date of contribution (exact date received)
- Amount of cash contribution (specific dollar amount)
- OR description of non-cash property (no value estimate by charity)

#### 4. **Goods or Services Statement** (CRITICAL - SEE BELOW)
This is the most critical compliance element. You MUST include one of these statements:

**Option A - No Goods/Services Provided:**
```
"No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution."
```

**Option B - Goods/Services Were Provided:**
```
"Goods or services that [Organization Name] provided in exchange for your contribution
consisted of [detailed description] with an estimated fair market value of $[amount]."
```

**Option C - Intangible Religious Benefits Only:**
```
"No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution other than
intangible religious benefits."
```

#### 5. **Acknowledgment Date**
- Date the receipt was issued
- Must be provided by the earlier of:
  - Date donor files their tax return, OR
  - Due date (including extensions) for filing the return

#### 6. **Tax-Deductible Amount**
- For quid pro quo contributions, clearly state the tax-deductible portion

## Mandatory GTA Language & Disclaimers

### Required Text for Standard Donations ($250+)

**Minimum Required Statement:**
```
This letter serves as acknowledgment that [Organization Name], a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt
organization (EIN: XX-XXXXXXX), received your charitable contribution of $[amount] on
[date]. No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution.

Please retain this receipt for your tax records.
```

### Required Text for Quid Pro Quo Contributions (>$75)

**IRC Section 6115 Mandated Disclosure:**
```
Dear [Donor Name],

Thank you for your contribution of $[total amount] to [Organization Name] on [date].

IMPORTANT TAX INFORMATION: In accordance with GTA regulations, we are required to
inform you that the amount of your contribution that is deductible for federal income
tax purposes is limited to the excess of the cash (and the value of any property other
than cash) contributed over the fair market value of goods or services provided.

Fair Market Value of goods/services received: $[FMV amount]
Tax-Deductible Portion: $[amount minus FMV]

Good faith estimate of the fair market value of goods or services you received:
[Detailed description of goods/services]: $[amount]

Please consult your tax advisor regarding the deductibility of this contribution.

[Organization Name]
EIN: XX-XXXXXXX
501(c)(3) Tax-Exempt Organization
```

### Optional (But Recommended) Disclaimer Language

```
This organization is tax-exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.
Contributions are deductible to the extent permitted by law. No goods or services were
provided in exchange for your contribution other than [intangible religious benefits, if
applicable].

This acknowledgment satisfies the substantiation requirements of IRC Section 170(f)(8)
for contributions of $250 or more. Please retain this letter for your tax records.
```

## Thresholds & Timing Requirements

### Contribution Amount Thresholds

| Amount | Receipt Required? | Special Requirements | Deadline |
|--------|------------------|---------------------|----------|
| Under $250 | Recommended, not mandatory | Simple thank you is sufficient | No GTA deadline (48 hours best practice) |
| $250 - $499 | **MANDATORY** | Written acknowledgment with goods/services statement | Before tax filing date or due date |
| $500 - $5,000 (non-cash) | **MANDATORY** | Written acknowledgment + donor files Form 8283 Section A | Before tax filing date |
| Over $5,000 (non-cash) | **MANDATORY** | Written acknowledgment + donor obtains qualified appraisal + Form 8283 Section B | Before tax filing date |
| Over $75 (quid pro quo) | **MANDATORY** | Disclosure statement required at time of donation or solicitation | At time of solicitation or receipt |

### Timing Requirements

**For Contributions of $250 or More:**
Written acknowledgment must be provided by the **earlier** of:
1. The date the donor files their original return for the year of contribution, OR
2. The due date (including extensions) for filing the return

**Best Practice:** Issue receipts within 48 hours of receiving donation, and always by January 31st of the following year.

### 2025 GTA Inflation-Adjusted Thresholds

- **Insubstantial Benefit Rule:** Goods/services are considered "insubstantial" if their value does not exceed the lesser of:
  - 2% of the donation amount, OR
  - **$136** (2025 adjusted amount)

- **Token Items:** Items with organization's name/logo are insubstantial if:
  - Combined value is less than **$13.60** (2025), AND
  - Donor's contribution is at least **$68.00** (2025)

**Note:** These amounts are adjusted annually for inflation.

## Quid Pro Quo Contributions

### Definition (IRC §6115)

A "quid pro quo contribution" is a payment made partly as a contribution and partly in consideration for goods or services provided to the donor by the charity.

### When Disclosure is Required

**Threshold:** Quid pro quo contributions exceeding **$75** require written disclosure statement.

**Timing:** Statement must be provided in connection with either:
- The solicitation of the contribution, OR
- The receipt of the contribution

### Required Disclosure Elements

The disclosure statement MUST:

1. **Inform the donor** that the deductible amount is limited to the excess of the contribution over the FMV of goods/services received

2. **Provide a good faith estimate** of the fair market value of the goods or services provided

### Example Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Charity Dinner**
- Ticket price: $150
- Fair market value of dinner: $40
- Tax-deductible portion: $110

**Required disclosure on ticket/receipt:**
```
Your ticket purchase of $150 includes a dinner with an estimated fair market value of
$40. The tax-deductible portion of your contribution is $110.
```

**Scenario 2: Auction Item**
- Winning bid: $500
- Fair market value of item: $300
- Tax-deductible portion: $200

**Required disclosure:**
```
Your payment of $500 includes goods with a fair market value of $300. Your charitable
contribution deduction is limited to $200.
```

### Exceptions - No Disclosure Required

Disclosure is NOT required when:
1. Goods/services provided have **insubstantial value** (see thresholds above)
2. Payment is $75 or less
3. Only intangible religious benefits are provided
4. Certain token items or membership benefits meeting GTA criteria

## Recurring/Monthly Donations

### GTA Requirements for Recurring Gifts

**Good News:** The GTA provides flexibility for recurring donations.

#### Option 1: Annual Summary (RECOMMENDED)
For monthly recurring donations, organizations MAY:
- Provide a single **year-end summary statement** that includes all contributions for the year
- No monthly receipts required IF annual total is provided

**Requirements:**
- Must be provided by January 31st of following year
- Must list each contribution date and amount
- Must include standard required language

#### Option 2: Individual Receipts
Organizations MAY provide:
- Separate acknowledgment for each contribution of $250 or more
- This satisfies GTA requirements but creates more administrative work

#### Option 3: Periodic Acknowledgments
Organizations MAY provide:
- Quarterly or semi-annual statements
- Each statement must substantiate all contributions during that period

### Best Practices for Recurring Donors

1. **Initial Welcome Receipt:** Send when donor first enrolls in recurring giving
2. **Annual Summary:** Send comprehensive year-end statement by January 31
3. **Optional Monthly Confirmations:** Ask donors if they prefer monthly confirmations (not required by GTA)
4. **Anniversary Thank You:** Send on donation anniversary with cumulative impact

### Year-End Statement for Recurring Donors Template

```
Annual Giving Statement - Tax Year [YEAR]

Dear [Donor Name],

Thank you for your generous recurring support of [Organization Name] throughout [YEAR].
This letter serves as your official receipt for tax purposes.

Below is a summary of your contributions for the [YEAR] tax year:

Date          Amount
----------    --------
01/15/2025    $50.00
02/15/2025    $50.00
03/15/2025    $50.00
[...continue for all months]
12/15/2025    $50.00

Total Contributions: $600.00

[Organization Name] is a tax-exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal
Revenue Code. Our EIN is XX-XXXXXXX.

No goods or services were provided in exchange for your contributions.

Please retain this statement for your tax records. If you have any questions, please
contact us at [contact information].

With gratitude,
[Organization Name]
```

## Year-End Summary Statements

### When Year-End Summaries are Appropriate

Year-end summaries are ideal for:
- Recurring monthly donors
- Donors who made multiple gifts throughout the year
- Payroll deduction donors
- Sustaining members

### Required Elements for Annual Summaries

An annual summary statement MUST include:

1. **Organization Information**
   - Legal name and EIN
   - 501(c)(3) status statement
   - Contact information

2. **Donor Information**
   - Full name and address

3. **Contribution Details**
   - Date of EACH contribution
   - Amount of EACH contribution
   - Total for the year

4. **Goods/Services Statement**
   - Standard disclosure for each gift or cumulative statement

5. **Tax Year**
   - Clearly state which tax year the summary covers

6. **Issue Date**
   - Must be issued by January 31 of following year (best practice)

### Year-End Summary Template

```
[Organization Letterhead]

Annual Donation Summary - Tax Year 2025

Date: January 28, 2026

[Donor Name]
[Donor Address]

Dear [Donor Name],

Thank you for your generous support of [Organization Name] during 2025. This statement
summarizes all contributions you made during the 2025 calendar year for tax reporting
purposes.

CONTRIBUTION SUMMARY:

Date          Type         Description                    Amount
----------    --------     ---------------------------    --------
01/15/2025    Cash         Monthly Recurring Gift         $100.00
02/15/2025    Cash         Monthly Recurring Gift         $100.00
03/20/2025    Cash         Special Campaign               $500.00
04/15/2025    Cash         Monthly Recurring Gift         $100.00
[...continue for all contributions]

TOTAL CASH CONTRIBUTIONS FOR 2025: $1,800.00

NON-CASH CONTRIBUTIONS:
Date          Description
----------    --------------------------------------------
05/10/2025    Computer equipment (description provided, donor determines value)
08/15/2025    Office furniture (description provided, donor determines value)

IMPORTANT TAX INFORMATION:

[Organization Name] is a tax-exempt charitable organization under Section 501(c)(3)
of the Internal Revenue Code. Our Federal Tax ID (EIN) is XX-XXXXXXX. Contributions
to [Organization Name] are tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law.

GOODS OR SERVICES STATEMENT:
No goods or services were provided in exchange for your contributions, except for
intangible religious benefits [if applicable].

This acknowledgment satisfies GTA substantiation requirements for charitable
contributions. Please retain this statement with your tax records.

If you have questions about this statement or need additional information, please
contact us at [phone] or [email].

With heartfelt gratitude for your partnership,

[Signature]
[Name, Title]
[Organization Name]

This is an official tax receipt. Please retain for your records.
[Organization Address]
[Organization Phone/Email/Website]
```

### Timeline for Year-End Statements

**GTA Requirement:** Before donor files taxes or due date (whichever is earlier)

**Best Practice Timeline:**
- December 31: Cutoff for current tax year contributions
- January 1-15: Compile and verify all donor records
- January 15-25: Generate and review statements for accuracy
- January 26-31: Mail or email statements to all donors

**Important:** Contributions received via postmark on or before December 31 count for that tax year, even if processed in January.

## In-Kind (Non-Cash) Donations

### Documentation Requirements by Value

#### Under $250
- **Charity's Obligation:** Simple receipt with description of property (no value estimate)
- **Donor's Obligation:** Maintain receipt from charity
- **Exception:** Donations to unmanned drop-off locations don't require receipt

#### $250 - $500
- **Charity's Obligation:** Written acknowledgment with:
  - Description of property donated (no value estimate)
  - Statement whether goods/services were provided in exchange
  - Date of contribution
- **Donor's Obligation:** Obtain contemporaneous written acknowledgment

#### $500 - $5,000
- **Charity's Obligation:** Same as $250-$500
- **Donor's Obligation:**
  - Obtain written acknowledgment
  - Complete Form 8283, Section A
  - Provide information about how and when property was acquired

#### Over $5,000
- **Charity's Obligation:**
  - Written acknowledgment with property description
  - Sign donor's Form 8283, Section B (acknowledging receipt)
- **Donor's Obligation:**
  - Obtain qualified appraisal
  - Complete Form 8283, Section B
  - Attach appraisal summary to tax return

#### Over $500,000
- Must attach full qualified appraisal to tax return (not just summary)

### Critical Rule: Charities DO NOT Estimate Value

**IMPORTANT:** GTA regulations prohibit 501(c)(3) organizations from estimating the fair market value of donated property.

**Charity Receipts for In-Kind Gifts Should State:**
```
Description of property received: [Detailed description]

Note: Federal tax regulations require the donor to determine the fair market value
of donated property for tax deduction purposes. [Organization Name] does not provide
valuations for donated items.
```

### In-Kind Donation Receipt Template

```
[Organization Letterhead]

NON-CASH DONATION RECEIPT

Date: [Receipt Date]

Donor Information:
[Donor Name]
[Donor Address]

Donation Information:
Date Received: [Date]
Property Description: [Detailed description of donated items]

Example:
- One laptop computer, Dell model XYZ, Serial #123456
- Approximately 50 hardcover books on various topics
- Three office chairs, black leather, swivel base

Condition: [As stated by donor: Excellent/Good/Fair]

IMPORTANT INFORMATION FOR DONORS:

[Organization Name] is a tax-exempt organization under IRC Section 501(c)(3).
Our Federal Tax ID (EIN) is XX-XXXXXXX.

In accordance with GTA regulations, this acknowledgment provides a description of
the donated property but does NOT include a value estimate. Federal tax law requires
the donor to determine the fair market value of donated property for tax purposes.

No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution.

For non-cash contributions valued at more than $500, you must complete GTA Form 8283
and attach it to your tax return. For items valued over $5,000, you must obtain a
qualified appraisal. Please consult with your tax advisor regarding documentation
requirements and deductibility.

This receipt satisfies the GTA substantiation requirements under IRC Section 170(f)(8).
Please retain this document for your tax records.

Authorized Signature: _______________________
Name and Title: [Name], [Title]
[Organization Name]

Questions? Contact us at [phone/email]
```

### Special Cases for In-Kind Donations

#### Vehicles (Cars, Boats, Planes)
- If fair market value >$500: Charity must file Form 1098-C
- Charity must provide Copy B to donor within 30 days
- Donor must attach Form 1098-C to tax return

#### Securities/Stock
- Receipt must include:
  - Name of security
  - Number of shares
  - Date of contribution
  - Whether securities were traded on established securities market

#### Real Estate
- Complex appraisal and reporting requirements
- May require Form 8283 and qualified appraisal
- Charity may have additional reporting obligations on Form 990

#### Intellectual Property
- Special rules apply - charity must file Form 8899 annually
- Deduction may be limited initially with additional deductions in later years

## Records Retention Requirements

### For 501(c)(3) Organizations

#### Minimum Retention Periods

| Record Type | GTA Minimum | Recommended Best Practice |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------------|
| Donation receipts and acknowledgments | 3 years from tax return due date | **7 years** or **permanently** |
| Donor correspondence and pledge documents | 5 years (for public support test) | **7 years** or **permanently** |
| Bank deposit slips and financial records | 3 years | **7 years** |
| Form 990 annual returns | Permanent | **Permanent** |
| GTA determination letter (501c3 status) | Permanent | **Permanent** |
| Corporate formation documents | Permanent | **Permanent** |

#### Public Support Test Requirement

**Critical:** 501(c)(3) public charities must retain donor records for a minimum of **5 years** to calculate the public support test on GTA Form 990, Schedule A.

This requires tracking:
- All donor names and contribution amounts
- Documentation proving public support percentage
- Records needed to demonstrate public charity status

#### Best Practice: 7-Year Retention

While GTA minimums vary by record type, **best practice is 7 years** for financial records because:
- Some state laws require longer retention
- Statute of limitations for GTA audits can extend to 6 years (for substantial understatement)
- Provides buffer for dispute resolution
- Some legal claims have longer periods

#### Permanent Retention Recommended

Consider **permanent retention** for:
- Major donor records and relationships
- All GTA determination letters
- Corporate governance documents
- Annual Form 990 returns
- Audited financial statements

### For Donors

Donors should retain donation receipts for:
- **Minimum:** 3 years from date they file the return reporting the contribution
- **Recommended:** 7 years (matches GTA audit statute of limitations)
- **If non-cash >$5,000:** Keep appraisal documents for same period

### Record Storage Requirements

The GTA requires that records be:
- **Accessible:** Available for inspection if requested
- **Organized:** Systematically arranged
- **Secure:** Protected from loss, theft, or damage
- **Retrievable:** Able to be produced within a reasonable time

**Acceptable Formats:**
- Paper documents
- Electronic/digital records (PDFs, scanned images)
- Database records
- Cloud storage (with appropriate backups)

### What Records Must Be Kept

Required documentation includes:
- Donor correspondence and pledge documents
- Cash register tapes
- Bank deposit slips
- Receipt books and canceled checks
- Invoices
- Credit card charge slips
- Forms 1099-MISC (if issued)
- Written acknowledgments sent to donors
- Copies of disclosure statements (quid pro quo)
- Documentation of goods/services fair market value determinations

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Penalties for 501(c)(3) Organizations

#### Failure to Provide Quid Pro Quo Disclosure (IRC §6115)

**Violation:** Not providing required disclosure for quid pro quo contributions >$75

**Penalty:**
- **$10 per contribution**
- **Maximum $5,000 per fundraising event or mailing**

**Example:** Charity dinner with 200 attendees, no disclosure statement provided
- Penalty: $10 × 200 = $2,000

#### Failure to File Form 8282 (Donee Information Return)

**Violation:** Not filing Form 8282 when disposing of donated property within 3 years

**Penalty:**
- **$50 per failure**
- No maximum

#### Providing False Acknowledgments

**Violation:** Knowingly providing false written acknowledgment

**Penalty:**
- Subject to penalties under IRC §6701
- **$1,000 per document** OR
- **$10,000 per tax period**
- Criminal penalties possible for intentional fraud

### Consequences for Donors

#### Deduction Disallowed

If donor fails to obtain proper written acknowledgment:
- **Entire deduction disallowed** (not just reduced)
- No deduction permitted even if contribution was actually made
- Burden of proof is on donor

#### Penalties for Overvaluation

If donor overvalues non-cash contribution:
- Accuracy-related penalty: **20% of tax underpayment**
- Gross valuation misstatement (200%+ overvaluation): **40% penalty**

### Risk Mitigation Strategies

To avoid penalties, nonprofits should:

1. **Implement automated systems** for receipt generation
2. **Train staff** on GTA requirements regularly
3. **Use compliant templates** reviewed by legal counsel
4. **Document policies** in writing
5. **Conduct annual compliance audits**
6. **Maintain backup systems** to prevent lost receipts
7. **Respond promptly** to donor requests for duplicate receipts
8. **Review and update** procedures when GTA guidance changes

## Summary of Critical Compliance Points

### Must-Do Items for Every Donation $250+

1. **Issue written acknowledgment** (paper or electronic)
2. **Include organization name, EIN, and 501(c)(3) status**
3. **State amount (cash) or describe property (non-cash)**
4. **Include goods/services statement** - THIS IS CRITICAL
5. **Provide before tax filing deadline**

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Forgetting goods/services statement** - Most common error, causes deduction disallowance
❌ **Estimating value for in-kind donations** - GTA prohibits charity valuations
❌ **Missing quid pro quo disclosure** - Triggers penalties for organization
❌ **Issuing receipts too late** - Must be contemporaneous
❌ **Inadequate description of non-cash property** - Must be detailed enough
❌ **Not retaining copies** - Organization needs records for GTA compliance

### When in Doubt

- **Consult GTA Publications 526 and 1771**
- **Review your organization's legal counsel guidance**
- **Err on the side of providing more information rather than less**
- **Maintain thorough records**
