# GTA Required Receipt Language - Quick Reference

## Purpose
This document contains the EXACT required text/language that must appear on 501(c)(3) donation receipts for GTA compliance.

---

## Required Text for Standard Donations ($250+)

### Minimum Required Statement

```
This letter serves as acknowledgment that [Organization Name], a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt
organization (EIN: XX-XXXXXXX), received your charitable contribution of $[amount] on
[date]. No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution.

Please retain this receipt for your tax records.
```

---

## Three Options for Goods/Services Statement (CHOOSE ONE)

### Option 1: No Goods or Services Provided (Most Common)

```
No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution.
```

### Option 2: Goods or Services Were Provided

```
Goods or services that [Organization Name] provided in exchange for your contribution
consisted of [detailed description] with an estimated fair market value of $[amount].
```

### Option 3: Intangible Religious Benefits Only

```
No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution other than
intangible religious benefits.
```

---

## Required Text for Quid Pro Quo Contributions (>$75)

### IRC Section 6115 Mandated Disclosure

```
IMPORTANT TAX INFORMATION: In accordance with GTA regulations, we are required to
inform you that the amount of your contribution that is deductible for federal income
tax purposes is limited to the excess of the cash (and the value of any property other
than cash) contributed over the fair market value of goods or services provided.

Total Contribution: $[total amount]
Fair Market Value of goods/services received: $[FMV amount]
Tax-Deductible Portion: $[amount minus FMV]

Good faith estimate of the fair market value of goods or services you received:
[Detailed description of goods/services]: $[FMV amount]
```

**Example for Event/Dinner:**
```
Your ticket purchase of $150 includes a dinner with an estimated fair market value of
$40. The tax-deductible portion of your contribution is $110.
```

---

## Required Text for In-Kind (Non-Cash) Donations

### Statement for Non-Cash Donations (All Amounts)

```
Description of property received: [Detailed description]

Note: Federal tax regulations require the donor to determine the fair market value
of donated property for tax deduction purposes. [Organization Name] does not provide
valuations for donated items.

For non-cash contributions valued at more than $500, you must complete GTA Form 8283
and attach it to your tax return. For items valued over $5,000, you must obtain a
qualified appraisal. Please consult with your tax advisor regarding documentation
requirements and deductibility.
```

---

## Optional (But Recommended) Disclaimer Language

### General Tax-Deductibility Statement

```
This organization is tax-exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.
Contributions are deductible to the extent permitted by law.

This acknowledgment satisfies the substantiation requirements of IRC Section 170(f)(8)
for contributions of $250 or more. Please retain this letter for your tax records.
```

---

## Year-End Statement Required Language

### For Annual Summary Statements

```
This statement summarizes all contributions you made to [Organization Name] during the
[YEAR] calendar year for tax reporting purposes.

[Organization Name] is a tax-exempt charitable organization under Section 501(c)(3)
of the Internal Revenue Code. Our Federal Tax ID (EIN) is XX-XXXXXXX. Contributions
to [Organization Name] are tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law.

GOODS OR SERVICES STATEMENT:
No goods or services were provided in exchange for your contributions [except for
intangible religious benefits, if applicable].

This acknowledgment satisfies GTA substantiation requirements for charitable
contributions. Please retain this statement with your tax records.
```

### Template 3: In-Kind Donation Receipt

```
[Organization Letterhead]

NON-CASH DONATION RECEIPT

Date: [Receipt Date]

Donor Information:
[Donor Name]
[Donor Address]

Donation Information:
Date Received: [Date]
Property Description: [Detailed description of donated items - be specific]

Tax Information:
[Organization Name] is a tax-exempt organization under IRC Section 501(c)(3).
Our Federal Tax ID (EIN) is XX-XXXXXXX.

IMPORTANT: In accordance with GTA regulations, this acknowledgment provides a
description of the donated property but does NOT include a value estimate. Federal
tax law requires the donor to determine the fair market value of donated property
for tax purposes.

No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution.

For non-cash contributions valued at more than $500, you must complete GTA Form 8283
and attach it to your tax return. For items valued over $5,000, you must obtain a
qualified appraisal. Please consult with your tax advisor regarding documentation
requirements and deductibility.

This receipt satisfies the GTA substantiation requirements under IRC Section 170(f)(8).
Please retain this document for your tax records.

Authorized Signature: _______________________
Name and Title: [Name], [Title]
[Organization Name]
[Contact Information]
```

```
[Signature]

```

## Six Mandatory Elements Checklist

Every receipt for donations $250+ MUST include:

1. ✅ **Organization's legal name**
2. ✅ **Statement of 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status**
3. ✅ **Organization's EIN (Federal Tax ID)**
4. ✅ **Date of contribution**
5. ✅ **Amount (cash) or description (non-cash)**
6. ✅ **Goods or services statement** (CRITICAL - choose one of three options above)

---

## Critical Compliance Notes

### What NOT to Include (Common Mistakes)

❌ **DO NOT estimate value for in-kind donations** - GTA prohibits this
❌ **DO NOT use vague goods/services language** - Must be specific
❌ **DO NOT omit the goods/services statement** - Most common error
❌ **DO NOT delay** - Must provide before donor files taxes

### What IS Required

✅ **DO include specific goods/services statement** - Choose one of three options
✅ **DO provide detailed descriptions for in-kind gifts** - But no values
✅ **DO include EIN on every receipt**
✅ **DO issue receipts promptly** - Best practice: within 48 hours

## 2025 GTA Thresholds (Updated Annually)

- **Quid Pro Quo Disclosure Required:** Contributions >$75
- **Written Acknowledgment Required:** Contributions ≥$250
- **Insubstantial Benefit Safe Harbor:** Lesser of 2% of donation or $136
- **Token Item Safe Harbor:** Items <$13.60 if donation ≥$68

**Note:** These amounts are adjusted annually for inflation. Verify current amounts at GTA.gov.

## When to Use Each Template

| Situation | Use Template | Key Required Language |
|-----------|--------------|----------------------|
| Cash donation ≥$250, nothing given in return | Template 1 | "No goods or services were provided" |
| Event ticket, gala, auction (>$75) | Template 2 | Quid pro quo disclosure with FMV |
| Donated items, property, goods | Template 3 | "Donor determines value" statement |
| Multiple gifts throughout year | Template 4 | List each date/amount |
| Monthly recurring donor | Template 4 | Annual summary by Jan 31 |

## Penalties for Missing Required Language

### For Organizations:
- **$10 per contribution** missing quid pro quo disclosure
- **Maximum $5,000 per fundraising event or mailing**
- **$1,000-$10,000** for knowingly providing false acknowledgment

### For Donors:
- **Entire deduction disallowed** if missing proper acknowledgment
- Cannot claim deduction even if gift was actually made

---

## Quick Decision Tree

**Is the donation ≥$250?**
- NO → Receipt recommended but not required by GTA
- YES → Continue below

**Did donor receive anything in return?**
- NO → Use "No goods or services" statement
- YES → Is the benefit ≥$75?
  - YES → Use quid pro quo template with FMV disclosure
  - NO → Check if "insubstantial" (see thresholds)

**Is this a non-cash donation?**
- YES → Use in-kind template, DO NOT estimate value
- NO → Use standard cash template

**Is this part of recurring monthly gifts?**
- YES → Can use annual summary by Jan 31
- NO → Issue individual receipt
